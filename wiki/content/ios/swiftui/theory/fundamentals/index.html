How SwiftUI itself thinks — the model underneath every screen you build. SwiftUI is declarative: you describe what the UI is for a given state, and the framework decides what to change on screen. To predict what it will do, you need three things: how it decides two views are the same view (identity), what a @ViewBuilder actually produces (construction), and how sizes are negotiated between parent and child (layout).

These pages are read once and apply everywhere. The State & Data Flow and Building Screens sections build directly on them.

Where to start: View Identity. Most SwiftUI bugs reported as "my state keeps resetting" or "my animation won't play" are identity bugs, and the vocabulary it establishes — structural versus explicit identity — is used by every other page in this domain.
