Two questions cover almost every state bug in SwiftUI: who owns this value, and how does it reach the view that needs it. Get ownership wrong and state resets at the wrong moment or outlives the screen it belonged to; get movement wrong and a child mutates a copy while the parent renders the original.

These pages assume the vocabulary from Fundamentals — in particular that identity, not value, decides whether state survives a redraw.
