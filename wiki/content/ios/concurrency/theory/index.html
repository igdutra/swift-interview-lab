Concurrency Theory

iOS · Swift Concurrency · Fundamentals · Isolation · Applied Patterns

Swift concurrency replaces a runtime discipline with a compile-time one. Under GCD, avoiding data races meant everyone remembering to take the right lock or hop to the right queue; under structured concurrency and actors, the compiler checks it. That shift is what makes the model worth learning properly rather than pattern-matching from the old one.

Two ideas do most of the work, and both invert a GCD assumption. A task is not a thread — it is an identity that the runtime schedules onto a small cooperative pool. And isolation, not threading, is what determines whether an access is safe.

Where to start: Task ≠ Thread. Almost every confusing behaviour in this area — a thread changing across an await, a cancellation check that seems to reach the wrong task, a deadlock from a semaphore — resolves once the execution model is clear.
