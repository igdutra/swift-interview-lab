Arrays & Hashing

Theory Hub · Hash Map · Two Pointers · Sliding Window · Prefix/Suffix

The first and most fundamental interview block: a handful of single-pass O(n) tools that solve the majority of array and string problems. Hash maps trade space for instant lookup; two pointers exploit sorted order to converge from both ends; sliding windows maintain an incremental summary of a moving range; prefix/suffix precomputation stores both sides of every index for O(1) queries. The Overview is the decision layer — how to pick the right tool and rule out the wrong ones — plus the Swift toolbox and cheat sheet; the deep dives below drill each technique against its problems.
